About

Against the Odds.
I Bet on Myself.

A bachelor's-level electrical engineering student at UMT, working across power systems, transformer engineering, control theory and applied AI — with a habit of documenting everything properly.

Background

How I got here

My journey into Electrical Engineering began with my family's transformer manufacturing business. Growing up surrounded by the practical aspects of power equipment sparked my initial interest and provided a unique perspective on the industry.

What started as a practical decision to follow the family tradition soon evolved into a genuine passion. Over time, I cultivated a deep enthusiasm for power engineering, transformers, electrical machines, and power systems, appreciating both their complexity and their impact on modern infrastructure.

Expanding beyond traditional engineering, I embraced software development, artificial intelligence, and research as complementary disciplines. These tools have broadened my approach to solving engineering challenges, enabling innovative and interdisciplinary solutions.

Engineering Principles

Engineering Principles

Understand Before You Build

Every engineering problem begins with understanding. Before designing a solution, I study how the system works, why it works, and where its limitations exist. Strong engineering starts with strong fundamentals.

Engineering Without Boundaries

Electrical engineering, software development, artificial intelligence, automation, and research complement one another. The most effective engineering solutions emerge where disciplines intersect.

Design. Validate. Document.

Every project should be technically correct, carefully validated, and thoroughly documented. Good documentation ensures engineering knowledge remains reproducible, maintainable, and valuable to others.
